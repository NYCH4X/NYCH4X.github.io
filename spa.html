    Central Park
    Central Park is a large park right in the center of New York City. Central Park is the most famous park in New York. It is located in the middle of Manhattan and is surrounded by famous buildings. Central Park is 4 kilometers long and covers an area of 341 hectares. The park is not only popular with tourists, but also with the local population. Approximately 25 million visitors come every year. The park was designed by Frederick Law Olmsted and Calvert Vaux. It contains multiple lakes, hiking trails, two ice skating rinks, a boating lake, an animal cemetery, open grass areas that are used for the practice of various sports or picnics for example and several playgrounds.There is also a small zoo in the park: Central Park Zoo. The zoo became famous because of the movie Madagascar.   
    Manhattan
    Manhattan is an island and is one of the five city parts of New York. Manhattan has 1.620.867 inhabitants and is has a size of 87,5 Squared Kilometers. about 28 of that consists of water. In 1626 Peter Minuit bought Manhattan planning to start New Amsterdam which evolved into New York. Manhattan is often referred to as ‘The City’ by New Yorkers since it is the most popular and densely populated of the five boroughs of New York. It is seen as the Center of the city both geographically and culturally. Iconic attractions like the Empire State Building, Statue of Liberty and Central Park are what makes Manhattan famous. Also the best restaurants and Broadway shows are to be found here. Times Square is one of the biggest cultural and fun places to visit in Manhattan. Also Wall Street is located in lower Manhattan and it is the known place for the stock markets. Off course you know the movie ‘the wolf of wall street’ and the TV-series ‘Gossip Girl’ which are filmed here. Manhattan should definitely be on all our bucket lists to visit some day.
    Times square
    Times square is a square near Manhattan. It is between Broadway and Seventh Avenue. It stretches from West 42nd Street to West 47th Street. It is a cultural square in the center of New York. The main characteristics of Times Square are the electronic billboards and advertisements. There are more than 50 electronic billboards.It is one of the most popular tourist places in the world. Almost most 330,000 people visit Times Square everyday and almost 50 million people have visited this place in Total. With new years eve this place is really crowded. A few minutes Before 00:00 a big Boll, the Times Square Ball is dropped slowly. This happens Every year.

    The Statue of Liberty and Liberty Island

    The Statue of Liberty is a sculpture on Liberty Island in New York Harbour in New York. The copper statue, the statue has been gifted to the United States by the French , was designed by the sculptor Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi and its metal framework was built by Gustave Eiffel. The statue was dedicated on October 28, 1886.

    The Statue of Liberty is a figure of Libertas, a robed Roman liberty goddess. She holds a torch above her head with her right hand, and in her left hand carries a tabula ansata inscribed in Roman numerals with "JULY IV MDCCLXXVI" (July 4, 1776), the date of the U.S. Declaration of Independence. A broken chain lies at her feet as she walks forward. The statue became an icon of freedom and of the United States, and a national park tourism destination. It is a welcoming sight to immigrants arriving from abroad.

    Bartholdi was inspired by a French law professor and politician, Édouard René de Laboulaye, who is said to have commented in 1865 that any monument raised to U.S. independence would properly be a joint project of the French and U.S. peoples. Because of the post-war instability in France, work on the statue did not commence until the early 1870s. In 1875, Laboulaye proposed that the French finance the statue and the U.S. provide the site and build the pedestal. Bartholdi completed the head and the torch-bearing arm before the statue was fully designed, and these pieces were exhibited for publicity at international expositions.

    The torch-bearing arm was displayed at the Centennial Exposition in Philadelphia in 1876, and in Madison Square Park in Manhattan from 1876 to 1882. Fundraising proved difficult, especially for the Americans, and by 1885 work on the pedestal was threatened by lack of funds. Publisher Joseph Pulitzer, of the New York World, started a drive for donations to finish the project and attracted more than 120,000 contributors, most of whom gave less than a dollar. The statue was built in France, shipped overseas in crates, and assembled on the completed pedestal on what was then called Bedloe's Island. The statue's completion was marked by New York's first ticker-tape parade and a dedication ceremony presided over by President Grover Cleveland.

    The statue was administered by the United States Lighthouse Board until 1901 and then by the Department of War; since 1933 it has been maintained by the National Park Service. Public access to the balcony around the torch has been barred for safety since 1916.
